# The Shattered Compact

## Chapter 1: The Fall of Ironmere

The fortress of **Ironmere** had stood for four centuries at the junction of the **Ashvale River** and the **Grey Ridge**. Tonight it was burning.

**Lord Edric Ashvale** sat at the centre of the ruin, a sword through his chest. General **Maren Holt** of the **Iron Compact** knelt beside him, pressing a cloth to a wound she already knew was fatal. Above them, the **Ironveil** — a sealed vault carved into the fortress bedrock — remained intact. It always did. Nothing opened the Ironveil but the **Compact Seal**, and the Seal had not been seen since the **Fracture War** thirty years ago.

**Master Cael Dorn**, the Compact's chief archivist, documented the battle from the northern tower. He had counted the casualties: three hundred dead, twelve missing, one prisoner of war — **Sera Donnach**, a spy from the **Veilwatch** faction who had been captured trying to reach the Ironveil before the battle began.

By dawn Cael had filed the death records. Edric Ashvale: killed in action at Ironmere. The Ironveil: sealed and intact. The **Compact Seal**: location unknown.

Maren Holt gave the evacuation order at first light. She handed command of the ruins to her adjutant, **Commander Pell Voss**, and rode east toward **Calder's Hold** with seven riders — including a young cartographer named **Tomas Brek** who had mapped every corridor of Ironmere twice over and still could not find the vault's secondary entrance that the old plans insisted existed.

The **Ashvale family crest** — three silver keys on black — was abandoned with the fortress.

## Chapter 2: The Veilwatch Compact

Fifteen days later, the city of **Vael** was quiet. **High Arbiter Solin** presided over the **Compact Council** from the Arbiter's Hall on **Cathedral Row**. Six seats. Three factions: the **Iron Compact**, the **Veilwatch**, and the neutral **Scholars of the Meridian**. Solin belonged to none of them.

Sera Donnach had been released in a prisoner exchange — two Veilwatch couriers for one archivist — and now sat opposite Master Cael in the Hall's lower gallery. She had a bruised jaw and very steady eyes.

"The Ironveil holds a **Meridian Compass**," she said. "The Scholars believe it can locate the Compact Seal."

Cael set down his pen. He had heard about the Compass. He had also heard it was lost during the **Siege of Pelworth** — a battle that had occurred *sixty* years ago, not thirty, a fact that mattered because it meant the Compass predated the Fracture War by a full generation. "The Compass was destroyed at Pelworth," he said. "I have the inventory records."

"Your inventory records are wrong." Sera placed a fragment of engraved bronze on the table between them. "This is from the Ironveil's outer seal ring. I pulled it from the rubble of the north wall before Maren's forces took me."

The fragment bore the Meridian symbol — a circle bisected by a horizontal line — and beneath it, in old script: *sealed, year 214*. The Fracture War had ended in year 198. Whatever was inside the Ironveil had been placed there sixteen years *after* the war.

Cael photographed the fragment. He did not tell Sera that he had found an identical symbol in Edric Ashvale's private correspondence — correspondence he had read the night before the battle, while Edric was still alive.

In the outer hall, Commander Pell Voss waited with a sealed message from **General Maren Holt**. Maren was at **Thorngate**, two hundred kilometres west, and the message was short: *The Ashvale heir has surfaced. Do not let the Council know until I reach Vael.*

## Chapter 3: The Ironveil Opens

Lord **Edric Ashvale** entered the Compact Council chamber without his guard.

High Arbiter Solin rose from his seat. The room went silent. Master Cael's hand moved to his document case — not for a weapon, but for the death record he himself had signed fifteen days ago.

"I was told you were dead," Solin said.

"Master Cael was mistaken." Edric's voice was hoarse — the wound, perhaps, or the road. He wore no Ashvale crest. He placed an object on the council table: a disc of black iron, engraved with three silver keys. The **Compact Seal**. "I have carried this since the Fracture War. My father gave it to me the day it ended. I did not declare it then because the Ironveil's contents were not yet my decision to make."

He turned to Sera Donnach. "You already know what's inside."

She nodded. "The Meridian Compass."

Cael cleared his throat. "The Compass was destroyed at the Siege of Pelworth."

"The Compass was *moved* to Ironmere before Pelworth," Edric said, "to protect it. What burned at Pelworth was a copy — the **False Compass** — built by the **Scholars of the Meridian** specifically to make the Veilwatch stop looking." He set the Compact Seal in the centre of the table. "My father knew. He sealed the vault the year after the Fracture War ended. Year two hundred fourteen."

Solin looked at the seal ring fragment Sera had placed beside it. The dates matched.

**Tomas Brek** appeared in the doorway, out of breath. He had ridden ahead of Maren's column and found — finally, from outside the north wall — the secondary entrance the old plans described. It was bricked over. The brick was fresh, laid within the last month.

Someone had already tried to open the Ironveil from the outside.

Commander **Pell Voss** drew his sword.
